1. Pleural effusion: This condition occurs when excess fluid builds up between the layers of the pleura, the thin membrane that lines the outside of the lungs and the inside of the chest wall. 2. Edema: This condition refers to swelling caused by excess fluid trapped in the body's tissues. Edema can occur in any part of the body, but it is most commonly observed in the hands, arms, feet, ankles, and legs. According to A5, it can be caused by a number of conditions, including heart, liver, or kidney disease, or from taking certain medications.
