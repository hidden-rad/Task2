In A4, we have the terms 'pleural effusion' and 'atelectasis'. These terms are medical conditions that are related to the lungs. 

Pleural effusion refers to the abnormal accumulation of fluid in the pleural space which is the cavity between the lungs and the chest wall. Atelectasis, on the other hand, is the collapse or closure of the lung resulting in reduced or absent gas exchange. It may affect part or all of one lung. It is a condition where the alveoli are deflated, as opposed to pulmonary consolidation.
